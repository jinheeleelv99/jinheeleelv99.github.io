About This Work

This work focuses on improving surgical guidance during microvascular decompression (MVD), a neurosurgical procedure that alleviates nerve compression by separating the offending blood vessel. One of the key challenges in MVD is identifying compression points along the depth axis, which are harder to detect than those in the horizontal plane. To address this, we developed a method that estimates accurate intraoperative distances using the Depth-Anything-V2 model, combined with the optical properties of standard surgical imaging. Unlike traditional approaches that rely on relative depth, our system provides absolute distance measurements with an error margin under 2 mm, validated against both intraoperative and preoperative references.

Pipeline Overview

Our pipeline estimates absolute intraoperative distances from monocular surgical video in microvascular decompression (MVD) procedures. We leveraged a pretrained monocular depth estimation model (Depth-Anything-V2), and converted the resulting relative depth maps into metric-scale distance values using microscope-specific calibration parameters. To determine meaningful points, we extract the centroids of key anatomical structures segmented by SAM2, and compute distances at those locations.

Method

We validated the geometric consistency of our estimated depth maps by aligning them with both preoperative 3D reconstructions from 3D Slicer and intraoperative surgical images.

Results

We estimated absolute distances between key anatomical structures using our depth model and compared them against intraoperative microscope measurements and preoperative 3D Slicer reconstructions. The predicted distances showed strong agreement with both references, with an average error of approximately 2 mm across cases.
